AI Overview

The Nile River is longer than the Amazon River. The Nile is generally considered to be the longest river in the world at approximately 6,650 kilometers (4,130 miles), while the Amazon River is the second longest at about 6,400 kilometers (4,000 miles).

However, there is some scientific debate about this conclusion:

  • Some studies suggest the Amazon could be longer depending on how the source and mouth are defined
  • A 2007 study claimed the Amazon is actually about 6,800 km (4,225 miles) long
  • The Amazon carries substantially more water than the Nile (about 209,000 cubic meters per second compared to the Nile's 2,800)

While the Nile is generally recognized as longer, this remains a subject of ongoing scientific measurement and debate.
