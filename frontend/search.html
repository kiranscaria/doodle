AI Overview

The Amazon River is the largest river in the world by volume, discharging about 209,000 cubic meters per second of water into the Atlantic Ocean. This represents approximately 20% of the Earth's freshwater entering the ocean. While the Amazon is often considered the second-longest river in the world, there is ongoing scientific debate about its length.

Key facts about the Amazon River:

  • It has the largest drainage basin in the world, covering about 40% of South America (approximately 7,050,000 km²)
  • Its length has been reported between 6,275 km (3,899 mi) and 6,992 km (4,345 mi)
  • A 2008 study by the Brazilian Institute for Space Research (INPE) suggested the Amazon is 140 km longer than the Nile
  • The Amazon carries substantially more water than any other river in the world (about 209,000 cubic meters per second compared to the Nile's 2,800)

While debate continues about whether the Amazon or Nile is longer, the Amazon is undisputedly the largest river by water volume, basin size, and discharge.
